I have built a production scheduling validation system that checks whether a natural-language requirement and Excel/CSV dataset are ready for CP-SAT optimization.

It uses Groq LLM to understand the requirement, map different sheet/column names, and validate key data like orders, inventory, machines, processing time, release time, and deadlines before solving.



USER_REQUIREMENT = (
    "Validate whether this automotive machining workbook has enough job order, "
    "part, finished stock, cycle time, workcell capacity, release time, and "
    "deadline data for CP-SAT scheduling. The goal is to complete all machining "
    "jobs as early as possible."
)

SCHEMA_CONTEXT = """PartRegister contains product master data for machined parts.
  part_code is the unique product identifier.
  cycle_time_min_per_piece is the machining processing time in minutes per unit.
  finished_goods_on_hand is the current stock quantity available.

  JobOrders contains customer job orders.
  work_order_id is the unique order identifier.
  part_code links each job to its part in PartRegister.
  qty_to_machine is the quantity the customer needs produced.

  CommitmentPlan contains scheduling constraints for each job order.
  work_order_id links each record to its job in JobOrders.
  release_day is the earliest day production can start.
  release_minute is the earliest time of that day production can start, in minutes.
  due_day_no is the deadline day by which the job must be completed.
  due_minute is the deadline time of that day, in minutes.

  WorkCells contains the production machines available for scheduling.
  workcell_code is the unique machine identifier.
  capacity_minutes_shift is the total working minutes available per machine per day.

  Relationships:
  JobOrders.part_code links to PartRegister.part_code.
  CommitmentPlan.work_order_id links to JobOrders.work_order_id."""
